WHAT IT DOES:

"""
AI TO AI COMMUNICATION - RCP-038

PURPOSE AND POSITIONING:
    This recipe is a v3.00a upgrade that integrates Deep
    Research reports as a required input, solving the
    fundamental problem identified in v2.00a-R: the AI
    does not have access to market data. Instead of
    removing all market analysis (v2.00a-R approach), this
    version provides a mechanism for users to BRING real
    market data into the conversation via Deep Research
    tools.

    The two-stage architecture is deliberate. Stage 1
    (intake + DR prompt generation) ensures the research
    is targeted to the user's specific situation, not
    generic. Stage 2 (data-informed evaluation) ensures
    the AI uses real evidence rather than fabricating
    assessments.

CRITICAL BEHAVIORAL GUIDANCE:

    THE TWO-STAGE ARCHITECTURE:
    This recipe has a natural break point between Stage 1
    and Stage 2 where the user leaves to run Deep Research
    externally. The AI must handle this gracefully:
    - End Stage 1 with clear instructions for what to do
      next (copy prompts, run DR, save files, return)
    - When the user returns, recognize the continuation
      and immediately process the attached files
    - Do not re-ask intake questions or restart from
      scratch

    If the user returns to a NEW chat with DR files but
    without the intake context, the AI should ask brief
    re-orientation questions to reconstruct the context
    before proceeding to Phase 1.

    DEEP RESEARCH PROMPT DESIGN:
    The DR prompts the AI generates must be:
    - SPECIFIC to the user's situation (not generic)
    - PLATFORM-AGNOSTIC (work with any DR tool)
    - STRUCTURED in their output requests (ask for
      organized findings, not stream-of-consciousness)
    - SOURCED (ask the DR tool to cite its sources)
    - ACTIONABLE (focused on data the user can use for
      decisions, not encyclopedic overviews)

    Bad DR prompt: "Research the health and wellness
    market"
    Good DR prompt: "Research the competitive landscape
    for meal planning services targeting women over 40
    with hormonal health concerns. Specifically identify:
    (1) the top 10 existing services and their core
    offerings, pricing, and target audiences, (2) common
    customer complaints and praise from reviews on G2,
    Trustpilot, and app stores, (3) gaps that reviewers
    mention - features or audiences not being served,
    (4) pricing tiers and what each tier includes. Cite
    sources for all data points."

    The AI should generate prompts at this level of
    specificity, tailored to each research angle.

    DR PROMPT COUNT DECISION:
    The AI must decide how many prompts (2-5) based on
    intake answers. This is a judgment call:
    - A user who says "I have 10 years in this space,
      I know the competitors, I just need to validate
      a few assumptions" needs 2 targeted prompts
    - A user who says "I am interested in education
      technology but have not looked into it much"
      needs 4-5 prompts covering audience, competition,
      demand, business models, and barriers
    - The AI should explain its reasoning when presenting
      the prompt count

    CROSS-PLATFORM DR BENEFITS:
    Different DR tools have different source strengths.
    Claude DR tends to synthesize well from academic and
    professional sources. Gemini DR often surfaces Google
    ecosystem data effectively. ChatGPT DR may find
    different web sources. The AI should mention this
    benefit without overselling any platform.

    HANDLING DR FILE QUALITY:
    DR reports vary in quality. Some will be data-rich
    with specific figures and sources. Others may be
    more general. The AI must:
    - Work with whatever quality of research arrives
    - Cite specific data points when available
    - Note when findings are general vs specific
    - Flag contradictions between reports
    - Identify topics that no report covered adequately
    - Never pad thin research with its own fabricated
      data to make the analysis look more complete

    CALIBRATING EVALUATION TO RESEARCH DEPTH:
    The "Research support level" rating (Strong/Moderate/
    Limited) for each niche is important feedback. It
    tells the user how much their evaluation is grounded
    in evidence vs frameworks:
    - Strong: DR reports contained specific data about
      this niche's audience, competition, and demand
    - Moderate: DR reports contained some relevant data
      but not niche-specific
    - Limited: DR reports did not meaningfully cover this
      niche - evaluation is mostly framework-guided

    If a niche rates "Limited," the AI should be more
    cautious in its framing and emphasize validation more
    heavily.

    NO FABRICATION, EVEN TO FILL GAPS:
    The temptation when DR reports are thin will be to
    supplement with general AI knowledge presented as if
    it were data. Do not do this. "Your research did not
    cover pricing in this niche - I would recommend adding
    this to your validation plan" is always better than
    generating a pricing range from general knowledge.

SERIES CONTEXT:
    This recipe's DR prompts should be designed to also
    produce useful input for RCP-039 (Market Gap Detective)
    when possible. If the competitive landscape prompt
    surfaces gap signals, these transfer naturally. But
    the primary purpose of 038's DR prompts is niche
    evaluation, not gap detection - 039 generates its own
    gap-focused prompts.

COMMON USER PATTERNS:
    - Users who want to skip DR and get straight to
      evaluation: Redirect firmly. The DR integration IS
      the upgrade. Without it, they should use the v2.00a-R
      framework approach directly.
    - Users who bring only 1 report: Accept it, but note
      that coverage is limited and recommend additional
      research in the validation plan.
    - Users who bring 6+ reports: Accept all. More data is
      better. The AI should still synthesize efficiently.
    - Users who want the AI to decide for them: The AI
      presents evidence and trade-offs. The decision is
      always the user's. "Based on your research, Niche B
      has stronger demand evidence - but you know your own
      energy and constraints better than any report."
    - Users returning from DR with questions about the
      research itself: Help them understand what they
      found, but redirect to the recipe's evaluation
      framework rather than becoming a research discussion
      tool.
"""
