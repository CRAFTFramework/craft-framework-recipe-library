"""
LOGIC CONSISTENCY AUDITOR — TLDR

WHAT IT DOES:
You paste any written content — a business plan, proposal,
report, strategy document, persuasive article — and the AI
analyzes its internal logical structure. It finds
contradictions, unstated assumptions, reasoning gaps, and
conclusions that overreach the evidence.

WHAT IT DOES NOT DO:
It does NOT check whether your facts are correct. A
perfectly logical argument built on wrong facts will pass
this audit. Use the Fact Verification Foundation recipe
(RCP-012) for factual accuracy. These two tools are
complementary — use both for thorough review.

HOW IT WORKS:
The recipe runs in three interactive phases:
1. STRUCTURE MAP — The AI reads your content and shows you
   its understanding of the logical skeleton. You confirm
   before it continues. This prevents the AI from analyzing
   the wrong thing.
2. GAP ANALYSIS — The AI identifies logic issues, quotes
   the specific passages involved, classifies severity, and
   asks probe questions. You discuss findings, push back
   where the AI missed context, and decide what matters.
3. REVISION GUIDANCE — For gaps you want to fix, the AI
   provides specific, actionable suggestions with examples.

KEY SETTINGS:
- Analysis Depth: "quick" for fast checks (top 3-5 issues),
  "standard" for comprehensive review, "thorough" for
  high-stakes content (adds counterfactuals and edge cases)
- Focus Area: "assumptions" / "connections" / "conclusions"
  or "all" for balanced analysis
- Content Structure: Let it auto-detect or tell it what
  type (argument, process, comparison, cause-effect,
  problem-solution) for faster, more targeted analysis

BEST FOR:
Business plans, investor pitches, strategic proposals,
persuasive articles, process documentation, comparison
analyses, or any content where a critical audience will
scrutinize your reasoning.

HONEST LIMITATIONS:
The AI analyzes text structure — it cannot know your full
context, your audience's expectations, or your strategic
intent behind certain simplifications. Some "gaps" it
finds may be intentional. The interactive format lets you
tell it so.
"""
