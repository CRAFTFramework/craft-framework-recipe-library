"""
TLDR RECAP - RCP-039 Market Gap Detective

WHAT IT DOES:
    Identifies specific gaps and opportunities within your
    chosen niche using Deep Research reports as evidence.
    First helps you build gap-focused research prompts,
    then uses your attached reports to generate data-
    informed gap hypotheses through three lenses, rate
    their evidence strength, and build an investigation
    agenda for real-world validation.

WHAT IT DOES NOT DO:
    - Does not investigate markets or access external data
    - Does not produce evidence-based gap analysis from
      its own knowledge
    - Does not assign opportunity sizes or confidence
      percentages
    - Does not provide trend data or demand metrics
      without DR reports as source
    - Does not replace talking to real people in your niche

HOW IT WORKS (Two Stages, 6 Phases):

    STAGE 1 - Research Preparation:
    Phase 0A: Intake Questions
        Choose quick (4 questions) or detailed (10
        questions). Share your chosen niche, market
        knowledge depth, known pain points, and existing
        observations about gaps.
    Phase 0B: Gap-Focused DR Prompt Generation
        AI analyzes your answers and generates 2-5 Deep
        Research prompts specifically designed to uncover
        gap signals: customer complaints, competitor
        weaknesses, underserved segments, unmet needs,
        and missing solution types. Prompts work with
        any DR tool. Session pauses.

    STAGE 2 - Data-Informed Gap Analysis:
    Phase 1: Research Intake
        Attach your DR reports. AI reads all files,
        extracts gap-relevant signals organized by
        audience gaps, need gaps, and solution gaps.
        Flags areas the research did not cover. You
        confirm the summary.
    Phase 2: Three-Lens Gap Hypothesis Generation
        Lens 1: Underserved audiences - who is being
        overlooked? Lens 2: Unmet needs - what problems
        are poorly solved? Lens 3: Missing solutions -
        what delivery types are absent? Each hypothesis
        includes DR evidence citation and signal strength
        rating (Strong/Moderate/Weak). WAIT gates between
        each lens for your feedback.
    Phase 3: Hypothesis Prioritization
        All hypotheses consolidated and organized by
        evidence strength. You select top 1-2 based on
        both evidence and your own energy and feasibility.
    Phase 4: Investigation Agenda
        For each selected hypothesis: community
        observation plan, competitor review analysis,
        direct conversation guide, demand signal checks,
        and a quick feasibility test. Includes both
        confirmation and disconfirmation signals.

KEY INPUTS REQUIRED:
    - Specific chosen niche (not broad categories)
    - Current market knowledge level
    - 2-5 Deep Research report files (any platform)

SERIES POSITION: 3 of 5 (Niche Discovery)
    037 (Passion-Skills) -> 038 (Niche Identifier) ->
    039 (this) -> 040 (Niche Persona Generator) -> related

HONEST POSITIONING:
    Gap hypotheses are educated interpretations of your
    research data, not confirmed market opportunities.
    Even with strong DR evidence, they remain hypotheses
    until you validate through direct investigation. The
    signal-strength rating helps you know which hypotheses
    have the strongest research backing.
"""

"""
AI TO AI COMMUNICATION - RCP-039

PURPOSE AND POSITIONING:
    This recipe is a v3.00a upgrade that integrates Deep
    Research reports as a required input for gap hypothesis
    generation. The v2.00a-R version correctly identified
    that the AI cannot investigate markets, but the
    three-lens framework was limited by having only the
    user's existing knowledge as input. With DR reports,
    the three lenses now operate on real data: customer
    complaints, competitor reviews, demand patterns, and
    market structure information.

    The critical upgrade: gap hypotheses are now tagged
    with signal strength (Strong/Moderate/Weak) based on
    how much DR evidence supports them. This gives users
    a reliable way to prioritize which gaps to investigate
    first.

CRITICAL BEHAVIORAL GUIDANCE:

    THE GAP-FOCUSED DR PROMPT DISTINCTION:
    RCP-038 generates DR prompts for niche EVALUATION
    (audience, competition, market size, viability).
    RCP-039 generates DR prompts for gap DETECTION
    (customer complaints, competitor weaknesses, unmet
    needs, missing solutions, underserved segments).

    The prompts should be clearly different even if the
    user runs them for the same niche. Gap-detection
    prompts should specifically ask for:
    - Negative customer reviews and common complaints
    - Feature comparison gaps between competitors
    - Forum discussions about what is missing
    - Audience segments that describe themselves as
      underserved or overlooked
    - Solution types that exist in adjacent markets but
      not in this one

    Bad gap DR prompt: "Research gaps in the meal
    planning market"
    Good gap DR prompt: "Analyze customer reviews and
    complaints about the top 10 meal planning services
    targeting women over 40. Specifically identify:
    (1) recurring complaints that appear in 3+ services'
    reviews, (2) feature requests that customers mention
    wanting but cannot find, (3) audience segments that
    describe themselves as poorly served (e.g., specific
    dietary needs, health conditions, budget constraints),
    (4) pricing complaints or value perception issues.
    Focus on review platforms (App Store, Google Play,
    Trustpilot, Reddit) and health/wellness forums.
    Cite specific sources."

    SIGNAL STRENGTH CALIBRATION:
    The three-tier signal system (Rule 12) is essential
    for honest gap assessment:

    STRONG SIGNAL:
    - Multiple DR reports mention this gap independently
    - Specific data points: "X% of reviews mention [Y]"
    - Named audience segments, specific complaints,
      concrete examples
    - The gap appears in both competitor analysis and
      customer feedback

    MODERATE SIGNAL:
    - One DR report addresses this with some specificity
    - General themes that could indicate a gap but lack
      concrete data
    - The gap appears in one data source but not
      confirmed by others

    WEAK SIGNAL:
    - No specific DR evidence, but general market
      patterns suggest this gap might exist
    - The AI recognizes a logical gap based on what IS
      covered (absence evidence)
    - User's hunch aligns with general knowledge but
      DR did not surface supporting data

    Always present the signal strength prominently. Users
    should know at a glance which hypotheses have real
    evidence behind them.

    THE THREE-LENS FRAMEWORK WITH DR DATA:
    Each lens now operates differently with DR input:

    Lens 1 (Underserved Audiences): The AI should look
    for audience segments mentioned in DR reports that
    are described negatively ("not for me," "does not
    work for people like me," "I wish there was something
    for [my situation]"). These are direct evidence of
    underserved audiences. The AI should also identify
    audience segments that appear in market sizing data
    but NOT in competitor targeting - these are
    structurally underserved.

    Lens 2 (Unmet Needs): The AI should extract pain
    points and complaints from DR reports that relate to
    UNSOLVED problems, not just poorly-solved ones. A
    complaint about a competitor's pricing is about the
    current solution; a complaint about "no one addresses
    [specific issue]" is about an unmet need. The
    distinction matters for hypothesis quality.

    Lens 3 (Missing Solutions): The AI should map the
    solution format landscape from DR data (what types
    of solutions exist: courses, tools, communities,
    coaching, products) and identify format gaps. Also
    check for price-point gaps, channel gaps, and
    accessibility gaps.

    HYPOTHESIS VS FINDING (REINFORCED):
    Even with DR evidence, a gap hypothesis is still a
    hypothesis. The DR reports themselves may contain
    errors, outdated information, or biased samples.
    The language must remain appropriately tentative:
    - "Your research suggests that [audience] may be
      underserved based on [evidence]"
    - NOT "Your research proves that [audience] is
      underserved"
    - "Review patterns indicate that [need] is not
      well-addressed" 
    - NOT "The data confirms that [need] is unmet"

    INVESTIGATION AGENDA WITH DR FOUNDATION:
    The v3.00a investigation agenda is more targeted than
    v2.00a-R because the DR data tells us WHERE to look
    and WHAT to look for. Instead of generic "observe
    communities," the agenda should specify which
    communities the DR reports identified, what search
    terms surfaced the most complaints, and which
    competitor reviews showed the strongest gap signals.

    The investigation focuses on what DR COULD NOT answer:
    - Are the complaints representative or outliers?
    - Is the underserved audience large enough to build
      a business around?
    - Would they pay for a solution?
    - Can you actually deliver what is missing?

SERIES CONTEXT:
    If the user ran RCP-038 first and brings those DR
    reports along with new gap-focused reports, the AI
    should use ALL available research. The 038 reports
    provide market context. The 039 reports provide gap
    signals. Together they give a more complete picture.

    The investigation agenda connects naturally to
    RCP-040 (Niche Persona Generator). If investigation
    confirms a gap, building detailed profiles of the
    underserved audience is the next logical step. The
    AI should mention this but always emphasize that
    investigation comes first.

COMMON USER PATTERNS:
    - Users who want to skip DR because they did it for
      038: Generate new gap-focused prompts. The 038
      prompts targeted niche evaluation, not gap
      detection. They can bring 038 reports as
      supplementary context, but gap-specific prompts
      will surface different intelligence.
    - Users with very strong existing knowledge: Their
      intake answers are rich data. The AI should
      generate fewer but more targeted DR prompts that
      focus on confirming or challenging the user's
      existing hypotheses rather than broad scanning.
    - Users who want every gap investigated: Redirect to
      prioritization. Investigating 1-2 strong-signal
      hypotheses deeply beats superficially checking 6.
    - Users who conflate DR evidence with confirmation:
      Gently reinforce that DR reports are secondary
      research. Real confirmation requires direct
      observation and conversation. The investigation
      agenda exists because DR alone is not enough.
    - Users whose DR reports reveal no clear gaps: Be
      honest. "Your research did not surface strong gap
      signals. This could mean the niche is well-served,
      that the gaps require deeper domain expertise to
      identify, or that the research questions need to be
      more targeted. Here are options..." Suggest either
      refined DR prompts or direct market observation as
      next steps.
"""
