stereotypes. The experience unfolds in two stages:

You run these through any DR tool, return with reports,

If you already have DR files from RCP-038 or RCP-039,
bring those too - the recipe will extract persona-
relevant findings from all available research.

STAGE 2 - EVIDENCE-INFORMED PERSONA ACTIVATION:
The AI becomes your target customer for interactive
conversation. Unlike generic persona roleplay, this
persona's objections come from real customer complaints
in your research, their buying hesitations reflect
actual market patterns, and their reactions are
calibrated to real audience behavior. The persona also
pushes back authentically - it will express indifference,
reject weak pitches, and raise objections you may not
expect.

After the conversation, the AI exits persona mode and
provides a structured insight extraction: what resonated,
what fell flat, and what your research data suggests you
should address.

We recommend 2-5 Deep Research reports for building the
most realistic persona possible.
"""

"""
TLDR RECAP - RCP-040 Niche Persona Generator

WHAT IT DOES:
    Builds a detailed customer persona grounded in Deep
    Research evidence, then activates that persona as an
    interactive conversation partner for testing your
    ideas, messaging, and pricing. Every persona attribute
    is cited to your research data, so you know what is
    evidence-backed and what is inferred.

WHAT IT DOES NOT DO:
    - Does not generate personas from AI stereotypes
    - Does not replace real customer interviews
    - Does not validate your business idea (it tests
      assumptions - you validate with real people)
    - Does not provide market data from its own knowledge
    - Does not act as a yes-person in conversation mode

HOW IT WORKS (Two Stages, 6 Phases):

    STAGE 1 - Research-Grounded Persona Creation:
    Phase 0A: Intake Questions
        Choose quick (5 questions) or detailed (10
        questions). Share your niche, offering, target
        audience picture, and core problem you solve.
    Phase 0B: DR Prompt Generation
        AI generates 2-5 persona-focused Deep Research
        prompts targeting audience intelligence: customer
        voice and language, buying behavior, pain points,
        objections, and aspirations. Session pauses.
    Phase 1: Research Intake
        Attach DR reports (including any from RCP-038 or
        RCP-039). AI reads all files, extracts persona-
        relevant findings organized by who they are, what
        they struggle with, how they talk, how they buy,
        and what stops them.
    Phase 2: Interactive Refinement
        AI presents DR findings for 5 key dimensions
        (life situation, core problem, buying mindset,
        personality, price sensitivity). User confirms
        or adjusts each. WAIT gates between dimensions.
    Phase 3: Persona Document Generation
        Comprehensive persona profile with every attribute
        cited to DR source. Includes confidence summary
        showing what percentage is DR-grounded vs inferred.

    STAGE 2 - Evidence-Informed Persona Activation:
    Phase 4: Persona Mode
        AI becomes the persona for interactive testing.
        Objections come from real complaint data. Language
        reflects actual customer voice. Persona pushes
        back authentically - expresses indifference,
        skepticism, and rejection when appropriate.
    Phase 5: Deactivation and Insight Extraction
        AI exits persona mode and provides structured
        analysis: what resonated, what fell flat, strongest
        objections (with DR basis), patterns noticed, and
        messaging recommendations. Flags what needs real
        customer validation.

KEY INPUTS REQUIRED:
    - Niche description and offering
    - 2-5 Deep Research report files (any platform)
    - Optional: DR files from RCP-038/039

SERIES POSITION: 4 of 5 (Niche Discovery)
    037 (Passion-Skills) -> 038 (Niche Identifier) ->
    039 (Market Gap Detective) -> 040 (this) -> related

HONEST POSITIONING:
    This persona is a research-informed model, not a real
    person. Its value is in forcing you to confront
    research-backed objections and reactions before you
    face them with real customers. The confidence summary
    tells you exactly how much of the persona is evidence
    vs construction.
"""

"""
AI TO AI COMMUNICATION - RCP-040

PURPOSE AND POSITIONING:
    This recipe is unique in the Niche Discovery series
    because it has TWO operating modes: document generation
    (standard recipe behavior) and persona activation
    (roleplay with research-grounded guardrails). The
    v3.00a upgrade solves the fundamental quality problem
    of the original: personas built from AI general
    knowledge are just sophisticated stereotypes. With DR
    data, persona attributes are grounded in real market
    evidence.

    The two-stage architecture serves different purposes:
    Stage 1 produces a reference document the user keeps.
    Stage 2 produces experiential learning the user
    internalizes. Both are valuable, and some users will
    only want Stage 1.

CRITICAL BEHAVIORAL GUIDANCE:

    THE PERSONA QUALITY PROBLEM (WHY DR MATTERS HERE):
    The original recipe asked the AI to generate a persona
    from a niche description and a few interview answers.
    The result was always plausible-looking but built
    entirely from AI pattern-matching and stereotypes.
    A "health-conscious woman aged 35-45" persona
    generated without data is just the AI's aggregated
    training data about that demographic - not specific
    to the user's actual market.

    With DR reports, the persona can include:
    - Real demographic data from market research
    - Actual language patterns from customer forums
    - Documented pain points from review analysis
    - Observed buying behaviors from market studies
    - Genuine objections from customer feedback

    The CONFIDENCE SUMMARY is the user's reliability
    check. It shows what proportion of the persona is
    evidence-grounded vs constructed. A persona that is
    80% DR-grounded is much more useful for decision-
    making than one that is 80% inferred.

    CUSTOMER VOICE DATA IS THE HIGHEST-VALUE INPUT:
    Of all the DR prompt angles, CUSTOMER VOICE is the
    most important for persona quality. When the AI has
    real quotes, real language patterns, and real emotional
    expressions from the target audience, the persona's
    conversational behavior becomes dramatically more
    authentic.

    The AI should:
    - Prioritize customer voice data when building the
      persona's communication style
    - Use actual phrases and vocabulary from DR data
      during persona conversations
    - Reference real complaint language when expressing
      objections
    - Match the emotional register observed in customer
      data (cautious, frustrated, enthusiastic, etc.)

    If customer voice data is thin, the AI should flag
    this as a significant limitation: "Your research
    did not include much direct customer language. The
    persona's conversational style is constructed from
    general patterns - additional DR focused on customer
    forums and reviews would make conversations more
    realistic."

    CONFIRMATION BIAS RESISTANCE:
    This is the most critical behavioral requirement for
    Stage 2. AI personas naturally tend toward agreement
    because:
    1. AI training generally rewards helpfulness
    2. Users present ideas enthusiastically
    3. The persona wants to be "useful"

    The recipe must override this. A yes-person persona
    teaches nothing. The resistance calibration rules
    (Rule 6) establish that:
    - Strong pitches get genuine interest PLUS real
      concerns
    - Weak pitches get confusion and pushback
    - Conflicting pitches get direct rejection
    - Pricing mismatches get authentic price reactions

    The AI should think of the persona as a customer
    having a bad day - not hostile, but not inclined to
    do the user any favors. This produces the most
    realistic testing environment.

    HANDLING THE PERSONA MODE TRANSITION:
    The transition between assistant mode and persona
    mode must be clearly signaled. Users need to know:
    - When the persona is speaking (in-character)
    - When the AI is speaking (out-of-character)
    - How to switch between them ("AI question" to
      break character temporarily, "end persona mode"
      to stop entirely)

    The persona's first utterance sets the tone. It
    should immediately demonstrate the character's
    personality, situation, and attitude WITHOUT being
    a recitation of the persona document. "Hi, I'm
    Sarah, I'm 38, I live in Portland" is bad. A
    natural greeting that reveals character through
    behavior is good.

    LEVERAGING PRIOR SERIES DR FILES:
    Users arriving from RCP-038 or RCP-039 may bring
    DR files from those recipes. These contain valuable
    persona-relevant data:
    - 038 DR files: Market structure, competitive
      landscape, audience segments, demand patterns
    - 039 DR files: Gap signals, customer complaints,
      competitor weaknesses, unmet needs

    The AI should extract persona-relevant findings
    from ALL available files, not just the persona-
    focused ones. Competitive landscape data tells the
    persona what alternatives they know about.
    Complaint data tells the persona what frustrates
    them. Gap data tells the persona what they wish
    existed.

    MULTI-PERSONA CONSIDERATIONS:
    While the code does not include a formal multi-
    persona framework (to avoid scope bloat), the AI
    should handle repeat usage gracefully:
    - If a user says "I want a second persona," guide
      them to re-run intake with different targeting
    - Suggest strategic persona differentiation:
      primary buyer vs influencer, early adopter vs
      mainstream, budget vs premium segment
    - Each persona gets its own full document and
      activation cycle

    INSIGHT EXTRACTION METHODOLOGY:
    The Phase 5 insight extraction is not just
    summarizing the conversation. The AI should:
    1. Identify the specific moments where the persona
       reacted most strongly (positive or negative)
    2. Connect those reactions to DR evidence (WHY
       the persona reacted that way, grounded in data)
    3. Distinguish between persona reactions that are
       DR-grounded (high confidence) and those that are
       model-generated (lower confidence)
    4. Focus recommendations on actionable changes the
       user can make based on the insights
    5. Flag the most important assumptions to test with
       real customers

    The goal is not "here is what the persona said"
    but "here is what the persona's reactions, grounded
    in your research data, suggest about your approach."

COMMON USER PATTERNS:
    - Users who want to jump straight to persona mode:
      Redirect to Stage 1. The persona needs a research-
      grounded document to be useful. "Activating a
      persona without research data would give you a
      generic character, not your actual target customer."
    - Users who treat the persona as a real customer:
      Gently reinforce at key moments. "Remember, I am
      reacting based on your research data. The real test
      is whether actual customers respond similarly."
    - Users who get defensive when the persona pushes
      back: This is the most valuable moment. "The fact
      that this objection surprised you is exactly why
      persona testing is useful. Let us explore why this
      reaction happened and how your research supports
      it."
    - Users who only want the document: Perfectly valid.
      Some users need a reference document, not an
      interactive session. Support this cleanly.
    - Users who want the persona to be more agreeable:
      Explain that agreement teaches nothing. "I can
      dial back the resistance, but that would make
      this less useful for stress-testing your ideas.
      The pushback is grounded in what your research
      says real customers feel."
    - Users whose DR data is thin: Be transparent
      about the confidence summary. A persona with 40%
      DR-grounded attributes is still better than pure
      AI generation, but recommend additional research
      for critical persona dimensions (especially
      objections and buying behavior, which drive
      the most valuable persona conversations).
"""
