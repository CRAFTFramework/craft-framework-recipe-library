   social media, review sites, or any external platform.
   If the user provides no feedback data, the AI must ask
   for it before proceeding.

2. PATTERN ANALYSIS: The AI reads feedback text and
   identifies patterns — recurring themes, sentiment
   clusters, frequently mentioned topics, language
   intensity. It does NOT perform statistical sentiment
   analysis or generate numerical scores from algorithms.

3. HONEST FRAMING: Observations are based on the sample
   provided. The AI should note when the sample is too
   small, too one-sided, or too narrow to support strong
   conclusions. A batch of 5 angry reviews is not
   representative of overall brand sentiment.

4. OPTIONAL PERSONA BUILD: If the user wants audience
   segmentation, the AI can identify behavioral clusters
   in the feedback and build profile sketches. These are
   hypotheses based on limited data, not validated

5. ACTIONABLE OUTPUT: Every analysis phase should connect

I will identify the major themes across all feedback,

- What the theme is about (in plain language)

- Whether it trends positive, negative, or mixed
- Specific language and phrases customers use
- Any sub-themes or variations

1. High frequency + high intensity (systemic and urgent)
2. High frequency + moderate intensity (systemic pattern)

- Patterns that need more data before acting
- Ambiguous feedback worth following up on
- Themes that might indicate emerging opportunities

MONITORING PRIORITIES:

- Suggested cadence for re-running this analysis

"""
CUSTOMER FEEDBACK ANALYZER — TLDR

WHAT THIS RECIPE DOES:
Helps you understand what your customers are saying about
you. You collect feedback — reviews, social posts, support
tickets, survey responses, whatever you have — paste it
in, and the AI helps you find the patterns: what people
love, what frustrates them, what keeps coming up, and
what to do about it.

WHAT YOU NEED:
- Customer feedback in text form (at least 20-30 pieces)
- Any format works: copied reviews, CSV exports, pasted
  posts, bullet lists
- Include source and date if you have them

THE AI DOES NOT:
- Monitor your social media
- Access review sites or any platform
- Generate sentiment percentages or scores
- Replace actual social listening tools

HOW IT WORKS:
1. AI assesses what you provided (enough data? diverse
   enough? any gaps?)
2. Identifies sentiment patterns (what's positive, what's
   negative, what's intense)
3. Extracts themes (the topics that come up most)
4. Gives you prioritized action recommendations
5. OPTIONAL: Builds audience segment sketches from the
   feedback patterns

After each step, the AI pauses and asks you a question.
It's a conversation, not a report dump.

BEST FOR:
Post-launch feedback analysis, quarterly brand check-ins,
understanding product issues, finding marketing angles
from customer praise, getting a structured read on what
customers are actually saying.

TIME: 30-60 minutes depending on volume
DIFFICULTY: Easy

NOTE: This recipe was originally part of a 4-recipe
"Social Listening" series. The other three were retired
because their territory is covered by other CRAFT recipes
(competitive analysis by RCP-004, personas by RCP-040/055,
trend forecasting by RCP-027).
"""

EXTENDED INFORMATION FOR AI — RCP-030 (CONSOLIDATED)
CUSTOMER FEEDBACK ANALYZER

PURPOSE OF THIS SECTION:
This section is read by the AI executing this recipe. It
provides execution guidance beyond what the code specifies.

--- THE FUNDAMENTAL CONSTRAINT ---

You CANNOT access social media. You CANNOT access review
sites. You CANNOT perform social listening. You are
reading text that the user has collected and pasted into
the conversation. Everything you know about this brand's
customer sentiment comes from the sample in front of you.

This means:
- You cannot verify whether the sample is representative
- You cannot check current sentiment trends
- You cannot compare against industry benchmarks
- You cannot access additional reviews or posts

Be transparent about these limitations. The user chose
to bring you this data — respect them by being honest
about what you can and cannot conclude from it.

--- DATA ASSESSMENT IS YOUR GATE ---

Phase 1 is the most important phase. Genuinely evaluate:

SAMPLE SIZE: Less than 10 pieces? Flag it. You can still
identify themes, but caveat every finding. 20-30 is the
minimum for meaningful patterns. 50+ is strong.

SOURCE DIVERSITY: If all feedback is from one source
(e.g., all 1-star reviews), the sample is inherently
biased. Flag this explicitly. "Your sample is entirely
from [source], which tends to attract [type] of feedback.
This gives us a good read on [specific issue] but may not
reflect overall brand sentiment."

TIME RANGE: If dates are provided, note the span. If
dates aren't provided, flag that you can't assess whether
patterns are current or historical.

SENTIMENT BALANCE: If the user pasted 30 negative reviews
and 2 positive ones, the "analysis" will obviously skew
negative. Note whether the sample appears curated or
comprehensive.

FORMAT QUALITY: Can you actually parse individual pieces
of feedback? If the user pasted a wall of text with no
separation, ask for reformatting before proceeding.

--- FALSE-PRECISION TRAPS ---

TRAP 1: Fabricating percentages. NEVER say "67% of
feedback is negative" or "sentiment score: 3.2/5." You
are reading text, not running algorithms. Say "The
majority of the feedback pieces you shared express
frustration" or "Roughly two-thirds of the feedback
focuses on [theme]."

TRAP 2: Claiming representativeness. NEVER say "your
customers feel that..." Say "In the feedback you shared,
the most common theme is..." The distinction matters —
feedback is a sample, not a census.

TRAP 3: Fabricating trends. Without dated data and
multiple time points, you cannot identify trends. If
the user asks "is sentiment improving?" and you only
have undated feedback, say so.

TRAP 4: Over-interpreting thin data. Three complaints
about shipping is a pattern worth noting. It is NOT
"a major brand crisis." Scale your language to the
evidence.

--- PHASE 5: PERSONA DEEP-DIVE ---

This phase is OPTIONAL. Offer it after Phase 4 with a
natural transition: "I could also look at this feedback
through a different lens — instead of organizing by
theme, I could try to identify distinct types of
customers based on their feedback patterns. Would that
be useful?"

If the user declines, move to the validation summary.

If the sample is too small (fewer than 15 pieces) or
too homogeneous (all from one type of customer), note
that persona segmentation would be unreliable and
suggest gathering more diverse feedback first.

When building segment sketches:
- Base EVERY attribute on actual evidence from the
  feedback. If you say "this segment values convenience,"
  point to the specific feedback that shows this.
- Do NOT fabricate demographic details. If nothing in
  the feedback indicates age, location, or income, don't
  invent these details.
- Label every sketch as a HYPOTHESIS, not a validated
  persona. "Based on these feedback patterns, one
  possible audience segment is..."
- Typically 2-3 segments is appropriate. More than 4
  from a small feedback sample is over-segmenting.

--- INTERACTIVE PACING ---

5 phases (6 with persona deep-dive). After each phase,
pause and ask ONE question.

Good pause questions:
- Data assessment: "Does this match your sense of the
  feedback you've been receiving? Is this a representative
  sample or did you select specific feedback?"
- Sentiment patterns: "Are there any themes here that
  surprise you, or does this match what you've been
  hearing day-to-day?"
- Theme extraction: "Which of these themes feels most
  urgent to your business right now?"
- Recommendations: "Do any of these recommendations
  connect to initiatives you already have underway?"
- Persona sketches: "Do these customer types feel
  realistic based on your experience?"

--- COMMON AI MISTAKES FOR THIS RECIPE ---

1. Pretending to have accessed social media or review sites
2. Fabricating sentiment percentages or scores
3. Treating a biased sample as representative
4. Over-interpreting small amounts of feedback
5. Generating generic brand advice unconnected to the
   actual feedback provided
6. Dumping all findings at once instead of working
   interactively
7. In Phase 5: inventing demographic details not
   evidenced in the feedback
"""
