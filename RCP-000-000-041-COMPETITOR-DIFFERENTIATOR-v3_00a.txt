"""
TLDR RECAP - RCP-041 Competitor Differentiator

WHAT IT DOES:
    Builds comprehensive competitive intelligence from
    Deep Research reports covering landscape positioning,
    content/SEO strategy, customer voice analysis, and
    competitive gaps. Synthesizes a differentiation
    strategy with messaging pillars, competitive responses,
    and a prioritized action plan. Consolidates the
    capabilities of four former recipes (041, 042, 043,
    044) into one DR-powered experience.

WHAT IT DOES NOT DO:
    - Does not visit competitor websites or read their
      content directly
    - Does not access SEO tools, review platforms, or
      social media analytics
    - Does not fabricate competitor strengths, weaknesses,
      rankings, or sentiment data
    - Does not replace competitive secret shopping or
      direct market experience
    - Does not score competitors numerically without
      DR evidence

HOW IT WORKS (Two Stages, 6 Phases):

    STAGE 1 - Research Preparation:
    Phase 0A: Intake Questions
        Choose quick (5 questions) or detailed (12
        questions). Share your business, niche, 3-5
        competitors, known strengths, and analysis goal.
    Phase 0B: Competitive DR Prompt Generation
        AI analyzes your situation and generates 2-5
        DR prompts targeting the most relevant angles:
        landscape positioning, content/SEO strategy,
        customer voice/reviews, pricing models, marketing
        tactics, competitive weaknesses. AI decides the
        mix based on your goals. Session pauses.

    STAGE 2 - Data-Informed Competitive Intelligence:
    Phase 1: Research Intake
        Attach DR reports (plus any from 038/039/040).
        AI reads all files, builds per-competitor
        intelligence profiles, identifies cross-competitor
        patterns and intelligence gaps.
    Phase 2: Multi-Dimensional Gap Analysis
        Three analysis layers with WAIT gates between:
        - Positioning gaps: where competitors leave space
        - Content/experience gaps: what they miss in
          delivery (if DR covers this)
        - Customer voice gaps: what their customers
          complain about (if DR covers this)
        All findings cite DR sources.
    Phase 3: Differentiation Strategy
        Primary differentiator (with demand evidence,
        competitor gap evidence, user fit, defensibility).
        Supporting differentiators. Positioning statement.
        Messaging pillars with proof points and customer
        language. Competitive response scripts.
    Phase 4: Action Plan
        Immediate/short-term/long-term actions. Monitoring
        plan. Validation agenda for unconfirmed assumptions.
        Series completion summary.

KEY INPUTS REQUIRED:
    - Business description and niche
    - 3-5 competitor names (websites if known)
    - 2-5 Deep Research report files (any platform)

CONSOLIDATED FROM:
    RCP-042 Competitive Landscape Analyzer (landscape
    angle in DR prompts + Phase 1 mapping)
    RCP-043 Competitor Content Gap Finder (content/SEO
    angle in DR prompts + Phase 2 content analysis)
    RCP-044 Competitor Customer Voice Miner (customer
    voice angle in DR prompts + Phase 2 voice analysis)

SERIES POSITION: 5 of 5 (Niche Discovery - Capstone)
    037 (Passion-Skills) -> 038 (Niche Identifier) ->
    039 (Market Gap Detective) -> 040 (Niche Persona
    Generator) -> 041 (this)

HONEST POSITIONING:
    Competitive intelligence is only as good as the
    research behind it. The AI structures your thinking
    and surfaces patterns in your data, but the DR
    reports provide the evidence. Your direct market
    experience and customer conversations validate it.
"""

"""
AI TO AI COMMUNICATION - RCP-041

PURPOSE AND POSITIONING:
    This recipe is the capstone of the Niche Discovery
    series and the most complex consolidation in the
    CRAFT recipe library. It absorbed three separate
    competitive intelligence recipes (042, 043, 044)
    because with DR integration, the distinction between
    "landscape analysis," "content gap finding," and
    "customer voice mining" becomes a distinction between
    DR prompt angles, not separate recipe workflows.

    The consolidation serves users better: instead of
    running four recipes with overlapping intake and
    redundant competitive research, they run ONE recipe
    that generates targeted DR prompts across all relevant
    angles and synthesizes everything into a unified
    differentiation strategy.

CRITICAL BEHAVIORAL GUIDANCE:

    THE PLATFORM ACCESS PROBLEM (CORE DESIGN DRIVER):
    All four original recipes assumed the AI could access
    competitor websites, read their content, check SEO
    rankings, and browse customer reviews. The AI cannot
    do any of this. This is not a limitation to work
    around - it is the fundamental reason for the DR
    integration. Deep Research tools CAN access these
    sources and produce reports the AI can read.

    The AI must NEVER:
    - Describe a competitor's website design quality
      without DR evidence
    - Assess a competitor's content strategy without
      DR evidence
    - Quote or paraphrase customer reviews without
      DR evidence
    - Estimate SEO rankings or keyword performance
      without DR evidence
    - Generate a comparison matrix with cells filled
      from general knowledge

    Every cell in every comparison, every competitive
    assessment, every customer sentiment claim MUST trace
    back to a DR report or be flagged as "not covered in
    your research."

    DR PROMPT DESIGN FOR COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE:
    Competitive DR prompts must be SPECIFIC to produce
    actionable intelligence:

    Bad: "Research competitors in the meal planning
    market"
    Good: "Analyze the competitive positioning of
    Mealime, PlateJoy, and Eat This Much in the meal
    planning market targeting women over 40 with hormonal
    health concerns. For each competitor, identify:
    (1) their stated value proposition and target audience
    from their website and marketing, (2) their pricing
    tiers and what each includes, (3) their content
    strategy: blog topics, publishing frequency, social
    media presence, (4) customer reviews from App Store,
    Google Play, and Trustpilot: top 3 complaints and
    top 3 praises with representative quotes, (5) their
    apparent SEO strategy: what terms they rank for, what
    content types they produce. Cite sources for all
    data points."

    The AI must generate prompts at this level of
    specificity, naming the actual competitors from
    intake and requesting structured, source-cited
    output.

    SELECTING COMPETITIVE ANGLES:
    The AI decides which DR prompt angles to recommend
    based on intake answers. The decision framework:

    Goal "landscape" → Always include: landscape
    positioning. Optional: pricing/business model.

    Goal "content_seo" → Always include: content/SEO
    strategy. Optional: landscape for context.

    Goal "customer_voice" → Always include: customer
    reviews and sentiment. Optional: competitive
    weaknesses for context.

    Goal "differentiation" or "all" → Include: landscape
    + customer voice minimum. Add content/SEO and
    pricing if user has enough competitors and scope.

    The AI should explain its angle selection reasoning
    when presenting prompts.

    HANDLING UNEVEN DR COVERAGE:
    DR reports will inevitably cover some competitors
    better than others and some angles more thoroughly
    than others. The AI must:
    - Work with whatever coverage exists
    - Be transparent about uneven coverage: "Your
      research covered Competitor A thoroughly but
      only briefly mentioned Competitor C"
    - Flag per-competitor intelligence confidence
    - Never pad thin coverage with fabricated
      assessments to make the analysis look balanced
    - Note coverage gaps in the action plan as areas
      needing additional research

    If one competitor has rich review data and another
    has none, the customer voice analysis should cover
    the data-rich competitor in depth and explicitly
    state what is missing for the others.

    THE GAP ANALYSIS STRUCTURE:
    Phase 2 presents gaps across three dimensions. Not
    all dimensions will have DR data. The AI should:

    - POSITIONING GAPS: Almost always possible if
      landscape DR was run. Map competitors on relevant
      dimensions, identify white space. This is the
      most reliable analysis.

    - CONTENT/EXPERIENCE GAPS: Only possible if DR
      covered content strategy. If not, skip this
      section and note: "Your research did not cover
      competitor content strategies. Consider running
      a content-focused DR prompt for this dimension."

    - CUSTOMER VOICE GAPS: Only possible if DR covered
      reviews. If not, skip and note the gap. If
      partially covered, analyze what is available and
      flag missing competitors.

    The AI should never force all three sections if the
    data does not support them. Quality analysis of what
    the data covers beats thin analysis stretched across
    all dimensions.

    DIFFERENTIATION STRATEGY GROUNDING:
    The Phase 3 differentiation strategy must pass TWO
    tests:

    Test 1 - Evidence test: Is the primary differentiator
    supported by DR evidence? Can I cite a report showing
    that competitors DON'T do this and customers DO want
    it? If not, it is a hypothesis, not a strategy.

    Test 2 - Authenticity test: Can the user actually
    deliver this? A differentiation strategy the user
    cannot execute is worse than useless. The AI should
    cross-reference the proposed differentiator against
    the user's stated strengths and capabilities.

    If the evidence is strong but the user's fit is
    questionable, present it honestly: "The research
    suggests strong demand for [X], but based on what
    you have shared about your capabilities, this would
    require significant investment in [Y]. Is that
    feasible for you?"

    COMPETITIVE RESPONSE SCRIPTS:
    The "Why not [Competitor]?" responses are among the
    most directly useful outputs. They must be:
    - Grounded in DR evidence (reference real competitor
      weaknesses, not invented ones)
    - Respectful (focus on your unique value, not
      competitor bashing)
    - Honest (do not claim advantages you do not have)
    - Specific (not generic "we offer better service"
      but "our customers get [specific thing] that
      [Competitor] does not offer, which addresses
      the [specific complaint] we saw in their reviews")

    SERIES CAPSTONE POSITIONING:
    Users who completed the full Niche Discovery series
    arrive at this recipe with:
    - RCP-037 output: Skills/passions alignment
    - RCP-038 DR files: Niche evaluation research
    - RCP-039 DR files: Gap identification research
    - RCP-040 output: Target customer persona

    The AI should leverage ALL available context. If
    the user brings prior DR files, extract competitive
    intelligence from them. The persona from RCP-040
    can inform which competitive dimensions matter most
    to the target customer. The gap analysis from
    RCP-039 can confirm positioning opportunities.

    Users arriving without prior series context get the
    full value of this recipe alone - it is self-
    contained. The series context enriches but is not
    required.

COMMON USER PATTERNS:
    - Users who want to analyze 10+ competitors: Redirect
      to 3-5 most relevant. Comprehensive analysis of 5
      beats surface-level analysis of 10. DR prompts
      become unfocused with too many targets.
    - Users who expect the AI to browse competitor sites:
      Explain clearly that this is what the DR step
      provides. "I cannot visit websites directly, but
      the Deep Research prompts I generated will have
      the DR tool analyze those exact sites and produce
      reports I can work with."
    - Users who bring only landscape data but want full
      differentiation: Work with what is available but
      note the gaps. "Your differentiation strategy is
      grounded in positioning data. Adding customer voice
      research would strengthen the messaging pillars
      significantly."
    - Users who want to skip straight to messaging:
      Redirect through the analysis. "Effective messaging
      must be grounded in competitive reality. Let us
      map the landscape first so your messaging targets
      real gaps."
    - Users whose competitors are very similar: This is
      actually the hardest scenario and the most valuable
      one. When competitors look alike, differentiation
      matters most. Focus on customer voice data for
      micro-level gaps, and experience/service model
      differences.
"""
